Privacy Ethics

Transparency is the foundation of our archive. These terms define how we handle the digital fragments you leave behind and our commitment to preserving the integrity of this personal space.

01 Terms of Reflection

By engaging with Echoes of Today, you are participating in a curated digital narrative. We ask that you respect the emotional labor and intellectual property contained within these pages. All content—unless explicitly stated otherwise—is the sole property of the author.

We encourage the sharing of "pieces" of this blog, provided they are accompanied by direct attribution. Commercial use of the prose, photography, or archival layouts is strictly prohibited without written consent.

02 Digital Fragments (Data Usage)

We collect only what is necessary to maintain the "Analog Archive" feel. This includes:

  • Voluntary identifiers provided during newsletter subscription.
  • Anonymous session fragments to understand which "pieces" resonate most.
  • Technical metadata to ensure the glassmorphic surfaces render correctly on your device.

Your data is never sold to third-party marketplaces. We treat your digital footprint with the same reverence we treat a physical journal.

03 The Right to be Forgotten

In alignment with the ephemeral nature of life, any user may request the complete removal of their digital interactions. Should you wish to withdraw your subscription or delete your commentary fragments, please reach out via our contact channels.

"The archive is not a permanent prison, but a temporary home for thoughts. You may leave at any time."

04 External Connections

Occasionally, we may point you toward external artifacts—books, galleries, or other archives. We do not control these spaces and are not responsible for their individual privacy practices. We recommend reviewing their terms before engaging.
